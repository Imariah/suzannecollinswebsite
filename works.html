The Hunger Games

From the #1 New York Times bestselling author Suzanne Collins comes a gripping tale of survival, sacrifice, and the unrelenting pursuit of freedom. In the post-apocalyptic nation of Panem, the glittering Capitol rules over twelve oppressed districts, keeping the people subdued with an annual spectacle of brutality: the Hunger Games. Twenty-four tributes are chosen—one boy and one girl from each district—to fight to the death in a televised arena, where alliances are as fragile as the lives at stake.

Katniss Everdeen never intended to become a symbol of defiance. But when her sister is chosen for the Games, Katniss volunteers in her place, thrusting herself into a harrowing battle for survival. Armed with her wits, her bow, and a fierce sense of justice, she becomes a beacon of hope in a world where hope has been extinguished. But the Games are just the beginning. As Katniss faces both physical and psychological battles, she discovers that her fight is not only for survival—but for the soul of a nation on the brink of rebellion. Thrilling, thought-provoking, and unforgettable, The Hunger Games is a story of courage in the face of tyranny, where the smallest spark can ignite a revolution.

Catching Fire

From the #1 New York Times bestselling author Suzanne Collins comes a thrilling sequel that delves deeper into the perils of rebellion, loyalty, and love. Catching Fire picks up where The Hunger Games left off, with Katniss Everdeen and Peeta Mellark returning to District 12 as victors. But their victory comes at a steep cost. The Capitol is watching their every move, and the seeds of dissent they unintentionally sowed during the Games have begun to sprout. With unrest spreading through the districts and President Snow's threats looming large, Katniss is forced to navigate a web of political manipulation and personal danger.

Just as the nation edges closer to chaos, Katniss and Peeta are thrust back into the arena for the Quarter Quell, a special edition of the Hunger Games designed to remind the districts of the Capitol’s power. Facing a deadly new cast of tributes, including past victors, and arenas more treacherous than ever, Katniss must rely on her instincts and the fragile alliances she forges. But this time, survival isn’t enough. As the stakes rise, she realizes the true battle is not just for her life, but for the future of Panem itself. Gripping, heart-pounding, and filled with unexpected twists, Catching Fire is a masterful exploration of defiance against tyranny and the cost of hope in a world ruled by fear.

Mockingjay

From the #1 New York Times bestselling author Suzanne Collins comes the explosive conclusion to The Hunger Games trilogy, a powerful tale of resistance, sacrifice, and the unrelenting fight for justice. Mockingjay follows Katniss Everdeen as she becomes the reluctant symbol of a growing revolution. Rescued from the Quarter Quell and now in the secret stronghold of District 13, Katniss grapples with the physical and emotional scars of her time in the Games. As the rebellion gains momentum, she is called upon to assume the role of the Mockingjay, a symbol of hope and defiance that unites the districts against the Capitol.

But being the Mockingjay comes with heavy consequences. Navigating propaganda, betrayal, and the personal toll of war, Katniss must confront the cost of her choices as her loved ones are drawn into the conflict. The Capitol strikes back with unrelenting brutality, forcing Katniss to face not only President Snow but also the darker sides of the rebellion she represents. As Panem spirals into chaos, she must decide who she can trust and what she’s willing to sacrifice to bring freedom to her people. Dark, emotional, and thought-provoking, Mockingjay is a harrowing yet deeply satisfying conclusion to a saga of courage and survival in the face of tyranny.
